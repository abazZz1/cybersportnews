What is CyberSport

Esports (also known as "computer sports" or "electronic sports", English esports) is a team or individual competition based on video games. It is recognized as an official sport in Russia. All esports disciplines are divided into several main classes, distinguished by the properties of spaces, models, a game task and the developed gaming skills of esports players: first-person shooters, real-time strategies, sports simulators, car simulators, flight simulators, fighting games, team role-playing games with elements of tactical and strategic games, etc.

Raffled prize funds can reach several million US dollars. The Dota 2 The International tournament has broken records for payouts several times: for example, $25 million was played in 2017, $26 million in 2018, and $34 million in 2019. In 2020, the championship was postponed due to the coronavirus pandemic. Tournament games are broadcast live on the Internet, attracting an audience of millions. For example, for the final of The International 2015, according to data from TrackDota.com , was watched by more than 4.6 million viewers, and in 2020, viewers spent more than 21 million hours on broadcasts of matches of the first division of the Russian EPIC League tournament.
